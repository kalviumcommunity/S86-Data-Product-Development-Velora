# CHURN REDUCTION INITIATIVE

## Executive Summary

### Situation

Customer churn has become one of the company's largest revenue challenges. The current churn rate is 7%, compared to the industry average of 4%, resulting in approximately $2 million in annual revenue loss. Our analysis identified customer support response time as the primary driver of churn.

### Key Findings

- Customers receiving support within 2 hours have a churn rate of only 3%.
- Customers waiting more than 24 hours experience a churn rate of 12%.
- High-value customers are the most affected by slow support and generate the highest revenue risk.

### Business Risks

- Annual revenue loss of approximately $2 million if no action is taken.
- Increasing customer dissatisfaction creates opportunities for competitors.
- Continued growth in support requests will further increase response times and churn.

### Recommendations

1. Hire two additional support engineers.
   - Cost: $200,000 annually
   - Expected ROI: 2x within the first year

2. Implement a 2-hour support response SLA.
   - Improves customer satisfaction.
   - Requires process monitoring.

3. Prioritize high-value customers.
   - Dedicated support queue.
   - Expected reduction in premium customer churn.

### Decision Needed

Approve the hiring budget and engineering resources before the next planning cycle to begin implementation.

### Next Steps

- Operations finalizes hiring plan.
- Finance approves budget.
- Engineering implements SLA monitoring.
- Customer Support begins rollout and performance tracking.
