# Supporting Evidence for Customer Churn Analysis

This document provides the supporting evidence for the key findings presented in the customer churn analysis narrative. Each finding is backed by one or more visualizations created during the business visualization analysis.

---

# Finding 1: Alpha Product Line Generates the Highest Revenue

## Supporting Evidence

**Chart:** `output/chart1_revenue_by_product.png`

- Alpha generated **$4,496,595** in Q4 revenue, making it the highest-performing product line.
- Beta followed with **$3,312,012**, while Gamma and Delta generated **$2,438,930** and **$1,477,960**, respectively.
- The chart annotation highlights Alpha as the **top-performing product**.

## Why It Matters

Alpha contributes the largest share of company revenue and should continue receiving investment in marketing, customer support, and product development. Lower-performing products such as Delta should be reviewed to identify opportunities for improvement or cost optimization.

---

# Finding 2: Revenue Shows a Consistent Upward Trend Throughout the Year

## Supporting Evidence

**Chart:** `output/chart2_revenue_trend.png`

- Monthly revenue for the top three products increased steadily during 2025.
- Alpha consistently outperformed Beta and Gamma every month.
- Revenue reached its highest point in **November 2025**, which is clearly highlighted in the chart.
- All three product lines remained below the trend threshold while maintaining positive growth.

## Why It Matters

The steady upward trend indicates healthy business growth and increasing customer demand. The November revenue peak suggests that successful seasonal campaigns or business activities can be replicated in future years to maximize revenue.

---

# Finding 3: Most Customer Orders Are Low to Medium Value

## Supporting Evidence

**Chart:** `output/chart3_order_value_distribution.png`

- Most customer orders fall within the **$0–$100** range.
- The median order value is **$92**, while the first and third quartiles are **$48** and **$170**, respectively.
- The histogram shows a right-skewed distribution, with relatively few very high-value orders.

## Why It Matters

Most customers place smaller orders, indicating opportunities to increase average order value through upselling, bundled products, or promotional offers. High-value purchases exist but occur much less frequently.

---

# Finding 4: Q4 Delivered the Strongest Quarterly Revenue

## Supporting Evidence

**Chart:** `output/chart4_revenue_composition.png`

- **2025 Q4** generated the highest total quarterly revenue of approximately **$3.41 million**.
- Alpha remained the largest contributor throughout all four quarters.
- Beta and Gamma also showed strong contributions, while Delta maintained a smaller but consistent share.
- The annotation highlights Q4 as the strongest-performing quarter.

## Why It Matters

Q4 represents the company's most successful sales period. Understanding the business activities, marketing campaigns, and customer behavior that contributed to this performance can help replicate similar success in future quarters.

---

# Finding 5: Marketing Spend Has a Strong Positive Relationship with Revenue

## Supporting Evidence

**Chart:** `output/chart5_marketing_vs_revenue.png`

- The scatter plot shows a **strong positive correlation (r = 0.93)** between marketing spend and revenue.
- Most observations closely follow the trend line, indicating that increased marketing investment generally produces higher revenue.
- One notable outlier appears in **September**, where marketing spending increased but revenue growth lagged behind expectations. This anomaly is highlighted in the chart.

## Why It Matters

Marketing investment is strongly associated with revenue growth, making it an effective driver of business performance. However, the September anomaly suggests that campaign effectiveness should be reviewed to ensure marketing budgets continue generating the expected return on investment.

---

# Overall Business Insight

The visual evidence consistently shows positive business performance across revenue growth, product contribution, and marketing effectiveness. Alpha continues to be the company's strongest product line, Q4 remains the highest-performing quarter, and marketing investment demonstrates a strong relationship with revenue generation. At the same time, opportunities exist to improve lower-performing product lines, increase average order values, and investigate campaign anomalies to further strengthen business performance.
